Star Brands Asia was founded in 2009 to launch and expand the best of niche beauty brands in Asian markets. Financially successful, the consultancy saw strong brand sales performance, and received strong reviews from a credible client brand list. The vision of the company has not changed from the beginning, albeit now the company is focused on China solely.

Rob Robertson
Founder & CEO
Rob has dedicated his career to successfully launching international consumer brand businesses in Mainland China and across the Asia zone. Fluent in Mandarin, Rob has worked professionally in the China for 13 out of the last 30 years. First as an import-export entrepreneur, then as a brand manager for P&G, then as marketing director for Clairol, then as general manager for L'Oréal's salon brand Matrix, then as the founder and CEO of Star Brands Asia, and finally most recently as the general manager of LVMH’s makeup brand Benefit Cosmetics, Rob has deep operational experience across all channels and all categories of beauty.
Jean Yin
CFO
Jean has developed her financial acumen through a variety of assignments including acting as Divisional Controller for LVMH Perfumes & Cosmetics Division. Since then, she has cofounded and developed two jewelry brands whose primary sales channels are online.
Linda Hu
General Manager
Starting her career as a product director for L'Oréal, Linda has led total launches for several indie beauty brands including Davines, Balmain and Caswell-Massey. She has been with SBA since 2009.
Maya Wang
Product Manager
Maya began as a Management Trainee with Benefit Cosmetics and was promoted to oversee Sephora business and Trade Marketing based on outstanding work on the Brand. She handles business development and is currently focused on the launch of MORPHE for SBA.
